    In short, graphic design commonly involves using both Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator, but they serve different purposes:

    1. Adobe Photoshop:
       - Primarily used for raster or bitmap graphics.
       - Ideal for photo editing, image manipulation, and digital painting.
       - Works well with photographs and images with complex shading and textures.
    
    2. Adobe Illustrator:
       - Primarily used for vector graphics.
       - Ideal for creating logos, icons, illustrations, and designs that require scalability without loss of quality.
       - Works with shapes, lines, and paths defined by mathematical equations.
    
Graphic designers often use both Photoshop and Illustrator, depending on the specific project's requirements and the type of graphics need.
